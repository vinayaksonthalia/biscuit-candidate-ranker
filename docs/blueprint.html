Dataset Defense 03/10

Defense-in-Depth Against Adversarial Profiles

The dataset contains intentional traps and noise. Our pipeline applies a multi-layered defense to isolate them.

1. Honeypots

Profiles with completely impossible timelines or inflated metrics designed to fool naive semantic search.

Hard Filtered (42 caught)
2. Keyword Stuffers

Candidates listing dozens of expert-level AI keywords (Pinecone, BERT) but carrying non-technical titles (Marketing Manager, Accountant).

Capped Score to 0.05
3. Services Careers

Candidates who have spent their entire career at consulting/IT services firms (TCS, Infosys, Accenture) representing a culture-fit risk.

Proportional Penalties

Architecture 04/10

Four-Stage Ranking Pipeline

  • Stage 0 (Stream Loader): Parses candidates line-by-line using standard Python generators. Eliminates high peak memory, scaling seamlessly to 100K+ profiles.
  • Stage 1 (Hard Filtering): Eliminates the 42 definitively impossible honeypots using strict deterministic rules.
  • Stage 2 (6-Dimension Scorer): Computes normalized sub-scores for each candidate. Applies suspicion multipliers to borderline cases.
  • Stage 3 (Deterministic Ranker): Sorts candidates descending by score and breaks ties ascending by candidate ID. Generates varied reasoning using MD5-based deterministic seeding.

Stage 1 Filter 05/10

Defensive Filtering & Borderline Suspicion

🚫 1. Hard Honeypot Filters (Caught: 42)

If a candidate meets any of these 3 impossible criteria, they are immediately discarded:

  • Zero-Duration Experts: Expert in 3+ skills with exactly 0 months of stated experience.
  • YoE Breakers: Stated duration of a single job exceeds total self-reported years of experience.
  • Timeline Breakers: Stated duration of a single job exceeds the calendar dates by more than 12 months.
⚠️ 2. Borderline Suspicion Penalty

For profiles that are highly suspicious but not provably impossible. They are penalized by a multiplier:

  • Borderline Zero-Duration (-0.15): Expert in 1-2 skills with 0 months of experience.
  • Minor Timeline Mismatches (-0.10): Stated job duration exceeds calendar span by 6-12 months.
  • Extreme Skill Durations (-0.05): Stated skill duration exceeds total career span by 2.5x.

Stage 2 Scorer 06/10

Composite Scoring Framework

Scores candidates on a scale of 0.0 to 1.0 across six core dimensions derived directly from the Job Description.

Career Relevance (30%)

Calculates ratio of time at product vs services companies. Checks titles for AI/ML keywords, searches descriptions for ML terms, and rewards ownership/shipping verbs.

Skills Match (25%)

Weights core must-haves (embeddings, vector search, evaluation metrics). Penalizes negative domain match (computer vision, robotics). Rewards skill assessments and peer endorsements.

Experience Band (15%)

Uses a smooth Gaussian-like curve centered on the 6-8 year ideal range. Evaluated against Robust YoE to prevent self-reported experience padding.

Behavioral Signals (15%)

Combines profile recency, recruiter response rate, profile completeness, GitHub activity score, open-to-work flag, and prior interview completion rate.

Location & Logistics (10%)

Rewards Noida/Pune residency. Considers relocation willingness. Scores notice period: buyouts for sub-30d get bonuses, while >90d notice periods are penalized.

Education Quality (5%)

Tier-1 (IIT, NIT, Stanford, etc.) and Tier-2 institution classification. Checks relevance of field of study. Applies heavy penalties for PhD wrong field matches.

Algorithmic Design 07/10

Key Innovations for Robust Alignment

1. Robust YoE Estimation

Self-reported years of experience is often inflated (e.g. 16.2 years vs. 8.17 years actual career span).

Our algorithm cross-validates self-reported YoE against the sum of dated career jobs. If they differ by >1.5 years, we trust the career history sum.

2. Exact Company Mapping

Consulting careers carry a heavy culture-fit penalty. Classifying companies via heuristics is prone to errors.

We fully mapped the 63 unique companies present in the candidate database, identifying exactly 52 product/tech companies and 11 consulting/services firms.

3. Active Builder Language

Participants often describe projects passively ("helped with", "assisted"). The JD requires active builders.

Our lexical parser detects active verbs ("shipped", "built", "architected", "deployed", "A/B tested", "at scale") to boost candidates with verified ownership.

Deterministic Outputs 08/10

Reproducible Rank & Reasoning

  • Strict Tie-Breaking: Sorts descending by score, ascending by candidate ID. Ensures unique, reproducible ranks (no random shifts).
  • No Network LLMs: Reasoning is generated deterministically on the local CPU without expensive network calls.
  • Deterministic Seeding: Computes an MD5 hash of the candidate ID to seed the randomizer per-candidate. The same candidate gets the same reasoning across runs, while ensuring variation across candidates.
  • Structured Variation: Uses 5 distinct sentence templates. Prioritizes strong facts (skills, career) over weak supporting details. Preserves proper noun casing (IIT, Meta).

Future Roadmap

  • Dynamic Score Calibration: Integrate a validation set of ranked candidates to automatically optimize dimensions weights (rather than heuristic tuning).
  • Semantic Parsing of Careers: Deploy a lightweight local model (e.g. a small BERT model running locally) to parse career histories for semantic concepts, replacing lexical matches.

💡 Key Learning

Building candidate discoveries requires looking past the keywords. By combining behavioral signals (availability) with career trajectory validation (robust experience, product context), we achieve a ranking that mirrors a professional recruiter's judgment, rather than an inflated skill list.
